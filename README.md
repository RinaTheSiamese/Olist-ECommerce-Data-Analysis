# Olist E-Commerce Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
In this project, I explored a Kaggle-sourced dataset from Olist (a major Brazilian e-commerce marketplace) to uncover what drives their sales and evaluate the performance of their delivery operations against their customers' and sellers' geographic locations.

## 🎯 Core Business Questions
This project was built specifically to answer the following questions:

**1. Revenue & Sales Trends:**
* What is the total (gross) revenue generated by Olist over time?
* What are the top 3 busiest days of the week for purchasing?

**2. Product Performance:**
* What are the Top 10 most popular product categories by total sales volume?
* Which product categories generate the highest average revenue per order?

**3. Customer & Seller Geography:**
* Which 5 Brazilian states have the highest number of customers?
* Is there a major discrepancy between where sellers and buyers are located?

**4. Logistics & Delivery:**
* What is the average delivery time (in days) from the moment an order is purchased to the moment it is delivered to the customer?
* What percentage of delivered orders arrived after their estimated delivery date?

## 🔍 What I Found
1. **Geographic Concentration vs. Volume:** The customer and seller bases share the exact same top three states of origin (São Paulo, Rio Grande do Sul, and Santa Catarina), however, the scale is vastly different. Sellers are overwhelmingly concentrated in São Paulo (accounting for 1,849 sellers compared to just 190 in the next highest state), while the customer base is significantly larger and more widely distributed across the map (with nearly 42,000 customers in SP alone).
2. **Delivery Network Performance:** Despite the logistical challenge of shipping from a highly concentrated seller hub in São Paulo to a broadly dispersed customer base, Olist's fulfillment operations are highly efficient. They maintain a **92% on-time delivery rate** and a very low **1% cancellation rate**, with packages taking an average of 12.5 days to arrive.
3. **Purchasing Trends & Seasonality:** Weekdays (Monday through Wednesday) drive the highest order volumes, while weekend purchasing drops significantly. In terms of product demand, the "bed bath table" category leads the marketplace in total sales volume (11.1K), followed closely by "health beauty" and "sports leisure". Additionally, the revenue trend line reveals a massive, singular spike in late November 2017, strongly indicating a highly successful Black Friday sales event.

<br>

## 💭 Personal Recommendations for Olist
Based on the dashboard findings, here are three actionable opportunities for the business:

1. **Incentivize Regional Seller Acquisition:** Since the customer base is already strong in Rio Grande do Sul (RS) and Santa Catarina (SC), but sellers are heavily bottlenecked in São Paulo (SP), Olist should run targeted campaigns to acquire more sellers in RS and SC. This would localize inventory and potentially reduce the 12.5-day average delivery time.
2. **Capitalize on November Seasonality:** The massive revenue spike in late November 2017 indicates that Olist's customers are highly responsive to Black Friday/Q4 sales. Marketing teams should allocate their highest ad spend in early November, specifically heavily promoting the top-converting "bed bath table" and "health beauty" categories.
3. **Investigate Freight Costs vs. Delivery Success:** While the 92% on-time delivery rate is great given the geographic spread, shipping out of a single SP hub to the rest of Brazil is likely driving up freight costs. The next analytical step should be investigating the average freight value paid per order to ensure profit margins aren't being sacrificed for delivery speed.
